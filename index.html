Providing hope for the hopeless.

That's what Dash of Hope stands for. For children who have lost their parents, or who simply have nowhere left to turn — we show up. Not as a charity handing out pity, but as a family that stays: with an education, a warm meal, and someone in their corner for good.

Our Mission

Lighting the Futures of Children in Need

Dash of Hope Corporation is committed to making a positive difference in the lives of orphaned and disadvantaged children. From classrooms to college to the family dinner table, we provide support, opportunities, and a brighter future — completely free of charge.

We believe no child should ever be denied an education, a warm meal, or a family who shows up for them. If this cause moves you, join hands with us.

High School Program

Rajiv Gandhi Memorial High School for Physically Handicapped Children — providing free residential education from Grade 1 through Grade 10.

About the Program

Education That Changes Everything

Children receive a full education from first through tenth grade following the government-prescribed syllabus. They stay in hostels and are provided complete boarding and lodging at no cost to their families.

Beyond academics, students are taught vocational skills including tailoring, basket weaving, and basic computer knowledge — building the skills they need for lifelong economic independence.

Many students go on to pursue higher education and secure government jobs, building a dignified life in society on par with the general population.

Rural Orphan Children Program

For a child who has already lost so much, we open our door and offer what every child truly needs — a warm meal, an education, and someone who will never let them face the world alone.

About the Program

A Home Where No Child Is Forgotten

In the villages surrounding Challagundla, many children have lost one or both parents and have nowhere left to turn. Dash of Hope welcomes them into a loving home, where they are fed, taught, and cared for as part of our own family — not as a charity case, but as a child who deserves every chance at a joyful childhood.

Each child receives free schooling alongside three nourishing meals a day, cooked with the same care and warmth as a mother's kitchen. Older children are gently taught basic cooking and everyday life skills, learning to look after themselves and their younger siblings in a home built on togetherness rather than survival alone.

Our caretakers are present through every fever, every bad dream, and every small victory — because a child growing up without parents should never have to grow up without love. Your support keeps this home running, one plate, one lesson, one hug at a time.

Our Story

About Dash of Hope

A nonprofit born from the belief that every child deserves a life filled with happiness, opportunity, and dignity.

Who We Are

Dash of Hope Corporation is a non-profit organization located in Sacramento, California, committed to making a positive difference in the lives of orphaned and disadvantaged children. We have dedicated ourselves to providing support, opportunities, and a brighter future for children in need.

Our Mission

At Dash of Hope, our mission is to empower children to thrive, dream, and achieve their fullest potential. We believe that every child deserves a life filled with happiness, opportunities, and inclusion. We undertake activities and support organizations carrying out this mission.

How We Operate

A Lean, Accountable Model

Dash of Hope is run by a small, dedicated team who partner directly with trusted local organizations already doing the work on the ground in Andhra Pradesh — rather than building a large overhead structure from scratch. That means your gift moves quickly and directly: toward hostel meals, tuition, school supplies, and the everyday costs of running a home for children who need one.

Some of our work, like the High School Program, is government-aided; other efforts, like the Junior College Program and the Rural Orphan Children Program, receive no outside funding at all and depend entirely on donors like you. We're happy to answer any questions about how funds are used — just reach out.
